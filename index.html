7-step framework — applied per role family

1

Intake the role like a market problem

Capture: business problem solved, true must-haves vs nice-to-haves, AI stack, research vs product vs platform orientation, comp reality, location flexibility, expected funnel size.

2

Build the talent map

Target companies, adjacent companies, academia/labs, open-source communities, internal pools, past applicants, silver medalists, conference ecosystems. Sourcing is market architecture, not just LinkedIn search.

3

Define channel strategy by role family

Direct sourcing, internal channels, community channels, event-based channels. Each family has a different channel mix — don't apply a single approach across all three.

4

Create role-specific sourcing plays

1-page playbook per family: what to look for, target companies, adjacent profiles, where to find them, messaging angle.

5

Craft messaging by role family

Researchers want craft and mission cited. Engineers want ownership and scale. Infra candidates want hard systems problems. One generic message serves none of them.

6

Track the funnel by channel and role family

Response rate, HM pass rate, interview conversion, source of hire. Move from "we sourced a lot" to "this channel produced quality for this family."

7

Document success stories

Before → action → outcome format. Quantify everything: response rate lift, days to slate, quality of hire. Build reusable playbooks from each win.

🔬 A · Research / Frontier AI

Conferences — where researchers surface

NeurIPS

Annual · Dec · Vancouver / virtual Family A

Largest ML conference. Target poster presenters, workshop speakers, and oral paper authors. Best sourcing window: Oct–Nov pre-conference CFP period.

ICML

Annual · Jul · Rotating global Family A

Core ML theory and methods. Target authors in LLM, RL, and optimisation tracks. Workshop co-organisers are senior researchers open to new relationships.

ICLR

Annual · May · Rotating global Family A

Highly selective — acceptance = strong signal. Outstanding paper and spotlight authors are top-1% targets.

CVPR

Annual · Jun · Rotating US Family A

Top computer vision venue. Critical for vision-language, video generation, and multimodal AI researchers. Focus on oral and best paper tracks.

ACL / EMNLP / NAACL

Annual · Rotating Family A

NLP-specific. Essential for LLM and alignment researchers. EMNLP attracts more applied/industry-adjacent talent than ACL.

AAAI

Annual · Feb · Rotating US Family A

Broad AI — planning, reasoning, knowledge representation. Useful for agents and neuro-symbolic researchers.

ECCV / ICCV

Biennial · Rotating Europe / global Family A

European / International computer vision. Strong for 3D, generative vision, and scene understanding. Alternates with CVPR in even years.

CoRL / RSS

Annual Family A

Robotics + ML. Small, tight-knit community — relationships matter more than cold outreach here.

🔬 A · Research / Frontier AI

What to look for

  • Published research — venues matter
  • LLM / RL / multimodal focus
  • First or second-author papers
  • Model training at scale
  • Novel architecture contributions
  • Collaboration with product teams

Adjacent profiles

  • PhD candidates — final year
  • Postdocs ready to transition
  • Research engineers with publications
  • Strong MS grads from top programs
  • Quant researchers moving into AI

Messaging angle

Lead with research craft and mission — not comp. Emphasise compute access, publication freedom, direct collaboration with product teams, and the specific unsolved problem. Name their paper. Do not send generic messages — researchers share bad outreach with each other.

Outreach template

Subject: Your work on [specific paper / topic]
Hi [name], I came across your paper on [specific topic] — the approach you took to [specific finding or method] is directly relevant to problems we're working on here. We're building out our research team focused on [research area]. The role involves access to [compute / dataset scale], publication freedom, and close collaboration with the product engineering team. Worth a 20-minute conversation to see if there's overlap?

Interview blockers & calibration fixes

Common blockers
  • HM insists on top-3 lab pedigree only
  • No publication bar defined — every profile debated
  • Comp too far below industry for senior researchers
  • Full paper presentation required — loop too slow
  • No research problem shared before screen
Calibration fixes
  • Define min venue bar upfront (top-tier conf or workshop)
  • Share a 1-page research problem brief before the screen
  • Separate researcher from research engineer track
  • Run a paper deep-dive as the core loop, not a whiteboard
  • Weekly HM calibration — review 3 profiles per session

HM calibration questions

What does "good research" look like here?
Clarify: published output vs applied innovation vs productisation speed. Forces HM to choose rather than say "all three."
What's the minimum publication venue bar?
Get a concrete answer — top-8 conf, workshop, pre-print, or none. Prevents arbitrary rejections later.
What's the collaboration model with engineering?
Researchers who've only published often underestimate product pressure. Surface this early.
Who is the closest current team member to the ideal hire?
Gives you a real talent anchor to map adjacent profiles against.

Monthly threads Family B

Model contributors, Space builders, dataset authors. HF community sprints are sourcing events in disguise.

Annual Family B

Ongoing Family B

Y Combinator AI batch alumni

⚙️ B · Engineering / Productization

What to look for

  • LLM integration — RAG, orchestration, evals
  • Production deployment experience
  • Vector DB / retrieval systems
  • Python + APIs at scale
  • Evaluation framework design
  • Experimentation mindset

Adjacent profiles

  • Backend engineers who shipped AI features
  • ML engineers from rec / personalisation
  • Full-stack builders of AI products
  • Solutions architects gone hands-on
  • Former founders with AI products

Messaging angle

Lead with scale and ownership — enterprise use cases, real-world impact, ability to shape platform architecture (not just feature work). Name the stack. Mention the production problem they'll actually solve.

Outreach template

Subject: Your [repo / post / project] — quick question
Hi [name], Came across your [GitHub project / blog post / Hugging Face model] — the way you approached [specific technical detail] is exactly the kind of thinking we need on our team. We're building [brief product context] and hitting real scale problems in [inference latency / eval pipelines / retrieval accuracy]. This isn't a feature-team role — it's platform-level ownership with a small, senior eng group. Open to a quick call this week?

Interview blockers & calibration fixes

Common blockers
  • HM rejects adjacent profiles without reading them
  • Loop includes irrelevant ML theory questions
  • Unclear AI Engineer vs ML Engineer track
  • Comp benchmarked against non-AI engineers
  • Take-home too long — top candidates drop off
Calibration fixes
  • Show HM 3 adjacent profiles before screening
  • Replace theory with a real system design task
  • Define the "must-have production AI" bar explicitly
  • Benchmark comp against AI-specific bands
  • Cap take-home at 2 hours with a clear scope brief

HM calibration questions

AI Engineer vs ML Engineer — what's the difference here?
Forces clarity: integration-heavy (RAG, evals) vs model-heavy (training, fine-tuning). Two very different talent pools.
What does "production AI experience" mean to you?
Some HMs mean any shipped feature. Others mean billions of requests. Define this before sourcing.
Would you consider a strong backend engineer who built LLM features?
If yes, opens the adjacent pool. If no, get the specific gap in writing.
What's the first project this person would own?
Converts vague role scope into a concrete story for outreach and interviews.

🏗️ C · Platform / Infrastructure

What to look for

  • Distributed systems at ML scale
  • GPU utilisation / CUDA / XLA
  • Model serving & inference optimisation
  • Training pipeline engineering
  • Data infra for ML (feature stores etc.)
  • Reliability / SRE mindset

Adjacent profiles

  • SREs transitioning to ML infra
  • Data engineers moving into ML platforms
  • Systems engineers with perf focus
  • HPC / scientific computing engineers
  • Compiler / runtime engineers

Messaging angle

Lead with technical depth and novel scale — inference latency at billions of requests, GPU cluster orchestration, training pipeline reliability. Name the actual scale and stack. Do not lead with mission or product vision alone.

Outreach template

Subject: Hard infra problem — think your background is relevant
Hi [name], Your work on [specific system / repo / talk / blog post] caught my attention — particularly how you handled [latency / scheduling / throughput challenge]. We're running into a genuinely hard problem in [inference serving at scale / GPU cluster scheduling / training pipeline reliability]. Small infra team, high ownership, direct path to shaping the platform architecture. This isn't a ticket-driven role. Would you be open to a 20-minute call?

Interview blockers & calibration fixes

Common blockers
  • JD reads like a generic SRE or DevOps posting
  • HM rejects SRE adjacencies without rationale
  • Interview tests systems theory, not ML systems
  • HPC candidates not considered at all
  • Comp benchmarked too low vs AI infra market
Calibration fixes
  • Rewrite JD around actual ML infra problems
  • Add SRE-to-MLOps as explicit "adjacent accepted" profile
  • Design interview around a real serving or scheduling challenge
  • Include HPC engineers in target company map from the start
  • Benchmark comp against MLOps / AI infra bands specifically

HM calibration questions

What's the hardest systems problem on the roadmap?
The answer becomes your sourcing brief and every outreach message. If HM can't articulate it, the intake isn't done.
Would you consider an SRE moving toward ML infra?
Unlocks the largest adjacent pool — get explicit buy-in before sourcing this profile.
Training infra vs inference serving vs data platform — what's the priority?
These are different talent pools. Force the ranking before sourcing starts.
What does success look like at 6 months?
Converts vague infra scope into a concrete outcome for outreach and the interview scorecard.
