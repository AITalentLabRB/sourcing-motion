What changes by family

Markets

  • A → frontier labs, top university labs, postdoc pipelines
  • B → AI-native startups, cloud platform teams, product orgs
  • C → hyperscaler infra, MLOps vendors, compute startups

Channels

  • A → ArXiv, conferences, co-author graphs, lab websites
  • B → GitHub, Hugging Face, HN, product launch posts
  • C → CUDA repos, MLSys / GTC, SRE communities, HPC networks

Conversion expectations

  • A → low volume, slow, high touch — response rates 15–35%
  • B → medium volume, moderate speed — response rates 25–45%
  • C → lower volume, specialist — response rates 20–35%

7-step framework — applied per role family

1

Intake the role like a market problem

Capture: business problem solved, true must-haves vs nice-to-haves, AI stack, research vs product vs platform orientation, comp reality, location flexibility, expected funnel size.

2

Build the talent map

Target companies, adjacent companies, academia/labs, open-source communities, internal pools, past applicants, silver medalists, conference ecosystems. Sourcing is market architecture, not just LinkedIn search.

3

Define channel strategy by role family

Direct sourcing, internal channels, community channels, event-based channels. Each family has a different channel mix — don't apply a single approach across all three.

4

Create role-specific sourcing plays

1-page playbook per family: what to look for, target companies, adjacent profiles, where to find them, messaging angle. See the Playbooks tab for all three.

5

Craft messaging by role family

Researchers want craft and mission cited. Engineers want ownership and scale. Infra candidates want hard systems problems. One generic message serves none of them.

6

Track the funnel by channel and role family

Response rate, HM pass rate, interview conversion, source of hire. Move from "we sourced a lot" to "this channel produced quality for this family." See the Funnel tab.

7

Document success stories

Before → action → outcome format. Quantify everything: response rate lift, days to slate, quality of hire. Build reusable playbooks from each win.

A · Research / Frontier AI

What to look for

  • Published research — venues matter
  • LLM / RL / multimodal focus
  • First or second-author papers
  • Model training at scale
  • Novel architecture contributions
  • Collaboration with product teams

Target companies

  • Frontier labs (OpenAI, DeepMind, Meta AI)
  • Hyperscaler research orgs
  • Top university labs (MIT, Stanford, CMU, Berkeley)
  • Well-funded AI research startups
  • National labs (where applicable)

Adjacent profiles

  • PhD candidates — final year
  • Postdocs ready to transition
  • Research engineers with publications
  • Strong MS grads from top programs
  • Quant researchers moving into AI

Messaging angle

Lead with research craft and mission — not comp. Emphasise: scale of compute access, publication freedom, direct collaboration with product teams, novel problem space. Name the specific research area and why it's unsolved.

Outreach template

Subject: Your work on [specific paper / topic]
Hi [name],

I came across your paper on [specific topic] — the approach you took to [specific finding or method] is directly relevant to problems we're working on here.

We're building out our research team focused on [research area]. The role involves access to [compute / dataset scale], publication freedom, and close collaboration with the product engineering team.

Worth a 20-minute conversation to see if there's overlap?

Interview blockers & calibration fixes

Common blockers
  • HM insists on top-3 lab pedigree only
  • No publication bar defined — every profile debated
  • Comp too far below industry for senior researchers
  • Full paper presentation required — loop too slow
  • No research problem shared before screen
Calibration fixes
  • Define min venue bar upfront (e.g. top-tier conf or workshop)
  • Share a 1-page research brief before the screen
  • Separate researcher from research engineer track
  • Run a paper deep-dive as the core loop, not a whiteboard
  • Weekly HM calibration — review 3 profiles per session

HM calibration questions

What does "good research" look like here?
Clarify: published output vs applied innovation vs productisation speed. Forces HM to choose rather than say "all three."
What's the minimum publication venue bar?
Get a concrete answer — top-8 conference, workshop, pre-print, or none. Prevents arbitrary rejections later.
What's the collaboration model with engineering?
Researchers who've only published often underestimate product pressure. Surface this early so you pitch the right people.
Who is the closest current team member to the ideal hire?
Gives you a real talent anchor to map adjacent profiles against.

Success story

Challenge

Senior Research Scientist role stalled — team only targeting 3 frontier lab alumni. Pipeline dry after 6 weeks.

Strategy & execution

Mapped co-author networks from recent NeurIPS papers. Added PhD-to-industry candidates from 4 top programs. Rewrote outreach to cite specific papers and connect to open problems.

Outcome

Response rate 12% → 34%. Slate in 18 days. Hired from postdoc pool — invisible to direct LinkedIn search.

  • Cloud platform AI teams
  • Enterprise product orgs with AI features
  • Developer tool companies
  • Fintech / healthtech ML teams

Adjacent profiles

  • Backend engineers who shipped AI features
  • ML engineers from rec / personalisation
  • Full-stack builders of AI products
  • Solutions architects gone hands-on
  • Former founders with AI products

Community & events

Messaging angle

Lead with scale and ownership — enterprise use cases, real-world impact, ability to shape platform architecture, not just feature work. Name the stack. Mention evaluation challenges or production problems they'll actually solve.

Outreach template

Subject: Your [repo / post / project] — quick question
Hi [name],

Came across your [GitHub project / blog post / Hugging Face model] — the way you approached [specific technical detail] is exactly the kind of thinking we need on our team.

We're building [brief product context] and hitting real scale problems in [inference latency / eval pipelines / retrieval accuracy]. This isn't a feature-team role — it's platform-level ownership with a small, senior eng group.

Open to a quick call this week?

Interview blockers & calibration fixes

Common blockers
  • HM rejects adjacent profiles without reading them
  • Loop includes irrelevant ML theory questions
  • Unclear split between AI Engineer and ML Engineer track
  • Comp benchmarked against non-AI engineers
  • Take-home too long — top candidates drop off
Calibration fixes
  • Show HM 3 adjacent profiles before screening — get written feedback
  • Replace theory with a real system design or debugging task
  • Define the "must-have production AI" bar explicitly
  • Benchmark comp against AI-specific bands
  • Cap take-home at 2 hours with a clear scope brief

HM calibration questions

AI Engineer vs ML Engineer — what's the difference here?
Forces clarity on whether the role is integration-heavy (LLM APIs, RAG, evals) or model-heavy (training, fine-tuning). Two very different talent pools.
What does "production AI experience" mean to you?
Some HMs mean any shipped feature. Others mean serving billions of requests. Defining this prevents good candidates being screened out for the wrong reason.
Would you consider a strong backend engineer who built LLM features?
If yes, opens the adjacent pool. If no, get the specific gap in writing so the sourcing team knows exactly what to rule in or out.
What's the first project this person would own?
Converts vague role scope into a concrete story you can use in sourcing messages and interviews.

Success story

Challenge

Applied AI Engineer role low traction — targeting only big-tech GenAI alumni. Response rate under 8% after 4 weeks.

Strategy & execution

Expanded to search, rec, and platform engineers who shipped LLM features. Built 150-company target map. Rewrote outreach around platform ownership and enterprise scale.

Outcome

Qualified response rate tripled. Stronger slate in less time. Hired from an adjacent pool that had been entirely overlooked.

C · Platform / Infrastructure

What to look for

  • Distributed systems at ML scale
  • GPU utilisation / CUDA / XLA
  • Model serving & inference optimisation
  • Training pipeline engineering
  • Data infra for ML (feature stores etc.)
  • Reliability / SRE mindset

Target companies

  • Hyperscaler infra / platform orgs
  • AI compute startups (Anyscale, Modal)
  • MLOps platform vendors
  • Fintech high-performance infra teams
  • Large e-commerce ML platform orgs

Adjacent profiles

  • SREs transitioning to ML infra
  • Data engineers moving into ML platforms
  • Systems engineers with a perf focus
  • HPC / scientific computing engineers
  • Compiler / runtime engineers

Channels

Primary

GitHub — CUDA / systems reposMLSys / GTC / KDD Infra engineering blogsLinkedIn Recruiter Systems conference proceedingsInternal infra teams

Community & events

Messaging angle

Lead with technical depth and novel scale — inference latency at billions of requests, GPU cluster orchestration, training pipeline reliability. These candidates want hard systems problems and strong eng culture. Name the actual scale and stack.

Outreach template

Subject: Hard infra problem — think your background is relevant
Hi [name],

Your work on [specific system / repo / talk] caught my attention — particularly how you handled [latency / scheduling / throughput challenge].

We're running into a genuinely hard problem in [inference serving at scale / GPU cluster scheduling / training pipeline reliability]. Small infra team, high ownership, direct path to shaping the platform architecture.

This isn't a ticket-driven role. Would you be open to a 20-minute call?

Interview blockers & calibration fixes

Common blockers
  • JD reads like a generic SRE or DevOps posting
  • HM rejects SRE adjacencies without a rationale
  • Interview tests distributed systems theory, not ML systems
  • HPC / scientific computing candidates not considered
  • Comp benchmarked too low vs. AI infra market
Calibration fixes
  • Rewrite JD around actual ML infra problems, not generic infra tasks
  • Add SRE-to-MLOps as an explicit "adjacent accepted" profile
  • Design interview around a real serving or scheduling challenge
  • Include HPC engineers in target company map from the start
  • Benchmark comp against MLOps / AI infra bands specifically

HM calibration questions

What's the hardest systems problem on the roadmap?
The answer becomes your sourcing brief and the centrepiece of every outreach message. If HM can't articulate it, the intake isn't done.
Would you consider an SRE moving toward ML infra?
Unlocks the largest adjacent pool. Get explicit buy-in before spending time sourcing this profile.
Training infra vs inference serving vs data platform — what's the priority?
These are different talent pools. Force the ranking before you start sourcing.
What does success look like at 6 months?
Converts vague infra scope into a concrete outcome — useful for outreach and the interview scorecard.

Success story

Challenge

ML Infra Engineer role open 11 weeks with no viable slate. JD written like a generic SRE posting. No adjacent backgrounds considered.

Strategy & execution

Rewrote intake around specific infra problems. Mapped SRE-to-MLOps transitions and HPC engineers from compute startups and national labs. Outreach led with the systems challenge, not the job title.

Outcome

First qualified slate in 12 days after reset. Two of three finalists from outside the traditional ML infra pool. Offer accepted by an HPC-to-ML transition hire.
